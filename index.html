Mary Mahoney

Geographic Information Systems Professional

About Mary

Mary is a GIS/Environmental Specialist who works with water utilities, local, state, federal government staff, and Virginia citizens to coordinate, collaborate, and educate about source water protection. Mary knows that spatial data is critical to characterizing risks in a watershed, and that water system data can be powerful in revealing trends and telling the story of an agency.

Mary has helped overhaul GIS mapping provided to water utilities and has brought GIS to staff in a user-friendly, online format to the Virginia Department of Health, Office of Drinking Water. She has previously supported GIS mapping operations in a consulting setting, where she gained experience in managing and delegating work to others. Mary has also provided presentations and trainings on her work - she rarely shies away from speaking to a crowd!

Mary is working towards a GIS Professional certification. Thanks to her diverse work experience, she has previously received wetland delineation training. She holds a Bachelors degree in Environmental Science from the University of Mount Union. In addition, Mary is currently working towards a Masters degree in Geographic Information Systems and Cartography – GIS Development Option from the University of Wisconsin-Madison.
